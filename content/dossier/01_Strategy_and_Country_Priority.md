# Strategy and Country Priority

## Decision rule
Use this plan as a budget-first, fit-first strategy, not a prestige-first strategy.

## Tier A countries
- Germany: low tuition, strong technical brand, strong ROI
- Taiwan: scholarship-friendly, technically strong, much better cost structure than US/UK
- Finland: high quality, but only worth it with fee waiver / scholarship
- South Korea: strong scholarship culture in technical universities
- New Zealand: excellent if you pivot into PhD / research-led study

## Tier B countries
- Singapore: excellent outcome, but very selective and often expensive for coursework
- UK: 1-year master’s efficiency is great, but only if funding is meaningful
- Australia: use mainly for scholarship / RTP / research routes

## Tier C countries
- US: apply only where assistantship / funding path is real
- Norway: public universities now charge tuition for most non-EU students and scholarships are weak

## Best route for your current profile
- 70% effort on master’s applications in low-cost / scholarship-aware countries
- 30% effort on funded research / PhD options

## Best route if your profile improves by Oct-Dec 2026
Increase PhD applications if you achieve at least two of the following:
- One paper / preprint / serious technical report
- One strong academic recommender from IIT Madras
- One research internship / professor-led project
- One strong IELTS/TOEFL result
